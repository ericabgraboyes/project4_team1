Predicting Customer Churn at European Banks:


This project's objective is to develop a machine learning model that can accurately predict if a customer will leave their bank. The concept of customer turnover or churn is paramount to most businesses as the cost of acquiring new customers is typically higher than the cost of retaining existing customers. We thought it would be interesting to understand what drives customer churn or turnover. That is to say are there certain demographics or characteristics in which a higher proportion of customers exited the bank vs. stayed. When training the model to predict customer turnover or churn, the project considered factors such as age, credit score, gender... [INSERT FACTORS BASED ON FEATURE IMPORTANCE]. Ultimately if banks understood what variables drove customers to leave they could not only implement measures to mitigate churn but also identify attributes that are important when implementing customer loyalty programs.


What was our approach?

We took a comprehensive approach to analyze European bank customer churn data and predict the likelihood of customers leaving the bank. Using SQL, Spark, and Scikit-learn, we performed data analysis and modeling tasks.

We started by extracting the relevant data using SQL and We performed data cleaning and preprocessing tasks with pandas to ensure the data was in a suitable format for analysis.

To predict customer churn, we utilized Scikit-learn, which offers a wide range of machine learning algorithms. We trained and evaluated several classification models such as LogisticRegression, KNeighborsClassifier, and RandomForestClassifier. We followed best practices by validating the models using appropriate techniques and evaluating metrics like accuracy.

Overall, our approach involved using SQL, Spark, and Scikit-learn to analyze the European bank customer churn data. By leveraging these tools, we aimed to gain insights into customer behavior and develop an accurate prediction model.
